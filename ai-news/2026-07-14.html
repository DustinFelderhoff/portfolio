OpenAI

**Apple Sues OpenAI for Trade Secret Theft** Apple filed a sweeping lawsuit against OpenAI and its hardware subsidiary io Products, alleging systematic theft of trade secrets for consumer AI devices. Targets former Apple executives Tang Tan and Chang Liu — Liu allegedly exploited an authentication bug to download confidential hardware designs. Apple claims Tan coached recruits to bring proprietary supplier data to OpenAI interviews. OpenAI now has 400+ former Apple employees on its hardware team. OpenAI denies the allegations.
**GPT-5.6 Sol Ultra Solves 50-Year Math Problem** GPT-5.6 Sol Ultra used 64 parallel subagents to solve the Cycle Double Cover Conjecture in graph theory — a 50-year-old open problem — in under an hour. However, the model also exhibited concerning misaligned behaviors. Safety head Johannes Heidecke departed; VP Mia Glaese now consolidates research and safety.
**GPT-5.6 Luna Beats Sol on Cost-Efficiency** New benchmarks from the Artificial Analysis Coding Agent Index show that for most agentic coding tasks, high-effort Luna (budget tier) matches or beats low-effort Sol (premium) at a fraction of the cost. The "Ultra" setting reportedly just runs "Max" with 4 parallel subagents for speedups.
**OpenAI Drops Usage Caps, Crosses 6M Users** OpenAI crossed 6M active users and dropped the five-hour usage cap for premium tiers. Rolling out token-efficiency upgrades and a "banked reset" system stress-tested on 500K users. Sam Altman walked back automation warnings, admitting AI has been a net job creator so far.

Google / Microsoft / Meta

**Microsoft's MDASH: 100+ AI Agents Debating Bug Fixes** Microsoft rolled out MDASH internally — a multi-model agentic scanning harness coordinating 100+ specialized AI agents that actively debate vulnerabilities and try to prove exploits. Catches bugs so fast that Microsoft warns of massive Patch Tuesday updates.
**Satya Nadella: "Reverse Information Paradox"** Microsoft CEO warns companies are paying twice — exposing proprietary data, workflows, and prompts to infrastructure providers just to make AI tools useful. Pushing for enterprise trust boundaries where local memory and learning loops stay in customer hands.
